More reasons to like the Nasdaq in September

By Kevin Marder

Published: Sept 4, 2014 3:13 p.m. ET

Share

By

KevinMarder

Kevin Marder is a guest columnist and a co-founder of MarketWatch. He is principal of Marder Investment Advisors Corp. and a contributor to The Gilmo Report. Previously, he served as chief market strategist for Ladenburg Thalmann Co. and developed institutional fixed-income risk management software for Capital Management Sciences.

On Wednesday, the Nasdaq Composite recorded its first distribution day of the current three-week advance. Volume was slightly above Tuesday's figure and 9% above average.

Institutions, which largely dictate the course of the market, have stepped up to the feeding trough over the past month by favoring large-capitalization growth issues. This is a substantial plus for general market health, even if the volume has not been overpowering due to the seasonal summer lethargy. The real tell will be to the extent that recent breakouts in these issues follow through.

Speculators should be prepared for more volatility in September than what was seen last month, especially to the downside. The chart below shows a reasonable area for the Nasdaq to pull back to in the event of a retracement

For a larger chart, please click here.

As was noted recently, since 1950, September is the worst-performing month of the year for the major averages. This fact notwithstanding, it is to be noted that eight of the last 10 Septembers have been up for the S&P 500, with one of the two down months being the 2008 crisis.

Of additional note: Since 1990, the September-October period has been the Nasdaq's second-best two-month period of the year, exceeded only by October-November.

Otherwise, a number of leading titles showed reversal action on Tuesday and/or Wednesday. These included Arista Networks ANET, +4.15% Depomed DEPO, +0.65% Greenbrier Companies GBX, -1.28% Illumina ILMN, +0.87% Invensense INVN, +2.51% JD.com JD, -4.06% Navigator Holdings NVGS, -0.27% Netflix NFLX, +0.64% Palo Alto Networks PANW, +2.04% Pacira Pharmaceuticals PCRX, +1.10% Sierra Wireless SWIR, +2.77% Truecar TRUE, +10.58% Under Armour UA, +0.78% Yelp YELP, +1.67% Zendesk ZEN, -0.88% to name 15.

Hot frack-sand plays Emerge Energy Services EMES, +2.76% and Hi-Crush Partners HCLP, +3.03% were not spared by the risk-off mentality.

Overall, with the exception of a few names like DEPO and INVN, the selling in numerous leaders can be described as mild, very similar to the distribution in the Nasdaq Composite itself.

Among the names, Athlon Energy ATHL, +0.70% is an oil and gas explorer and producer in Texas' Permian Basin and in New Mexico. Most analysts who follow the stock on Wall Street look for earnings to grow by 78% in 2014 and 80% in 2015. Revenue growth has been impressive, up 98%, 93%, and 109% in the past three quarters, respectively. Of particular note is the most recent quarter's 29% jump from the prior quarter.

The number of mutual funds that sponsor the shares has risen from 165 funds to 199 to 218 to 255 over the past few quarters, respectively.
