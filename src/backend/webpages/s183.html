Andre Ellington limited in practice

Updated: September 7, 2014, 1:01 AM ET
By Josh Weinfuss | ESPN.com

  • Comments0

TEMPE, Ariz. -- Arizona Cardinals starting running back Andre Ellington will be a game-time decision after being limited in Saturday's practice, coach Bruce Arians said.

He was officially listed as questionable on the Cardinals' final injury report of the week after getting an MRI on Thursday on a foot injury.

[+] Enlarge
Jennifer Stewart/USA TODAY SportsAndre Ellington was limited in practice Saturday and remains a game-time decision for Monday night's opener against the Chargers.

Ellington returned from missing Friday's practice to take part Saturday. Arians, who kept his answers on Ellington short, said the second-year back is moving well.

"For him, everything is good," Arians said.

Ellington practiced enough Saturday to show Arians what he needed to determine the running back's status for the team's opener against the San Diego Chargers on "Monday Night Football." Arians wouldn't expand on what exactly he saw in Ellington but it was enough to indicate Ellington was ready to play.

"There's no doubt," Arians said.

Arians isn't concerned about Ellington's foot being a long-term issue.

"It's one of those things that every day when you wake up it could be different," Arians said.

Also listed as questionable for the Cardinals is defensive back Tyrann Mathieu, who is coming back from torn knee ligaments.
