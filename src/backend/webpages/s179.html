Game balls awarded by NFL Nation reporters:

WEEK 1

FALCONS 37, SAINTS 34 | ANALYSIS
Brandin Cooks. Rookie receiver Brandin Cooks found a way to exceed the lofty hype in his NFL debut. He caught seven passes for 77 yards and a touchdown and ran the ball once for 18 yards. Cooks will be yet another matchup nightmare for the loaded Saints. -- Mike Triplett
Matt Ryan. The Falcons veteran played like an elite quarterback in setting a franchise record with 448 passing yards. He completed 31 of 43 passes with three touchdowns and no interceptions while extending plays with his feet -- yes, his feet. -- Vaughn McClure
VIKINGS 34, RAMS 6 | ANALYSIS
Cordarrelle Patterson. It has to go to Patterson, who became the first Vikings wide receiver to rush for 100 yards, as he burned the Rams on a couple of jet sweeps in the first half and lined up in the backfield for a 67-yard touchdown run late in the third quarter. Patterson finished with 102 yards on three carries. -- Ben Goessling
Brian Quick. Pickings were incredibly slim here anyway, regardless of which side of the ball you look at. But receiver Brian Quick was the lone bright spot on the offense and finished with seven catches for 99 yards. The third-year wideout at least flashed the promise of better things ahead, which is more than can be said for most of the offense. -- Nick Wagoner
STEELERS 30, BROWNS 27 | ANALYSIS
Le'Veon Bell. He was the Steelers' best offensive player on a day Ben Roethlisberger and Antonio Brown went for more than 300 yards passing and 100 yards receiving, respectively. Bell finished with just under 200 rushing and receiving yards combined, and he would have had two scores had a holding penalty not nullified an early touchdown run. -- Scott Brown
Mike Pettine. Give this one to the guy in charge. Pettine watched his team play dismally the first two quarters, then did something about it. In the end, the Browns turned a debacle into a near-miraculous comeback. They did not finish the job, but without some sort of leadership from the top, this game remains a blowout. -- Pat McManamon
EAGLES 34, JAGUARS 17 | ANALYSIS
Darren Sproles. We'd give it to Nick Foles, but he would have dropped it. Let's go with Sproles, whose 49-yard touchdown run and two 22-yard punt returns got the Eagles back into the game in the third quarter. -- Phil Sheridan
Allen Hurns. He cooled off after his hot start, but it was still an impressive day for the rookie. He caught four passes for 110 yards and two touchdowns. He is the first player in NFL history to have two receiving touchdowns in the first quarter of his NFL debut. -- Michael DiRocco
JETS 19, RAIDERS 14 | ANALYSIS
Jets' defensive line. Motivated by a quote from former teammate Austin Howard, who said this past week the Raiders were going to push the Jets around, the defensive line held Oakland to 25 rushing yards, tipped three passes and recorded a sack. -- Rich Cimini
Greg Olson. His unit did not play well (nothing worked well for Oakland), but the game ball goes to Oakland offensive coordinator Greg Olson. He stayed with the team and coached after his father, Ken, a longtime high school coach in Washington, died Wednesday. It had to be a trying week for Olson. -- Bill Williamson
BENGALS 23, RAVENS 16 | ANALYSIS
A.J. Green. The receiver caught the winning, 77-yard touchdown with 4:58 remaining in the game. With the score and a subsequent two-point conversion, the Bengals went from trailing 16-15 to leading 23-16. Green caught six passes for 131 yards off nine targets in the game. -- Coley Harvey
Justin Forsett. The seven-year journeyman took advantage of Bernard Pierce getting benched and ran for 70 yards on 11 carries. His first touchdown since November 2012 moved the Ravens to within 15-7 in the third quarter. Forsett likely earned the start for Thursday night's game. -- Jamison Hensley
BILLS 23, BEARS 20 | ANALYSIS
Brandon Marshall. He caught a team-high eight passes for 71 yards and a touchdown and showed plenty of grit and toughness doing it. Marshall suffered what appeared to be an ankle injury on the final play of the third quarter, while making a 7-yard reception. He would return to the sideline on a couple of occasions before he came back and finished the game. -- Michael C. Wright
Kyle Williams. The defensive captain came up with a critical interception in the fourth quarter, as he tracked Bears quarterback Jay Cutler on a scramble and got his 300-pound frame in front of the pass. That stalled a potential Bears scoring drive and allowed the Bills to take the lead on an ensuing field goal. -- Mike Rodak
TEXANS 17, REDSKINS 6 | ANALYSIS
Alfred Morris. The running back provided the only consistent threat offensively. Morris did a good job of setting up his cutbacks and gained 91 yards on 14 carries. Yes, he was involved in a fumble, but it was a rough handoff, so it's tough to put the blame on Morris. He did get solid help from his blockers, but only when Morris could cut back inside. -- John Keim
J.J. Watt. Five days after the Texans made him the highest-paid defensive player in the history of the NFL, Watt repaid his employers. Among his exploits Sunday were a sack, a fumble recovery, two tackles for loss, a blocked extra point and a batted pass, and on one play he forced Robert Griffin III into an intentional grounding penalty. -- Tania Ganguli
TITANS 26, CHIEFS 10 | ANALYSIS
Eric Berry. The strong safety had a team-leading 15 tackles with one pass defensed and a quarterback hit. He will have to continue to lead the Chiefs secondary. -- Adam Teicher
Jake Locker. Cornerback Jason McCourty was a candidate with two interceptions for the Titans, but we've got to go with quarterback Jake Locker. He was efficient and smart. Locker finished 22-for-33 for 266 yards with two touchdowns, no picks, four sacks and a passer rating of 111.4. -- Paul Kuharsky
DOLPHINS 33, PATRIOTS 20 | ANALYSIS
Jamie Collins. On a day when it was a challenge to find a player who was a difference-maker throughout, linebacker Jamie Collins forced a fumble and also had good coverage in the end zone when the Patriots needed a stop in the fourth quarter. -- Mike Reiss
Knowshon Moreno. The Miami running back provided a spark off the bench with 134 rushing yards and a touchdown on 24 carries. Moreno should soon overtake Miami starting tailback Lamar Miller, who fumbled Sunday and struggled at times, for a starting job. -- James Walker
PANTHERS 20, BUCCANEERS 14 | ANALYSIS
Derek Anderson. It has to be Anderson. He said he could do almost everything Cam Newton does, and he almost did. He scrambled when he needed to, sneaked for 2 yards on fourth-and-1 from the Tampa Bay 5 and showed great precision in completing 24 of 34 pass attempts for 230 yards and two touchdowns. -- David Newton
Jorvorskie Lane. With a 54-yard run, the fullback accounted for more than half of Tampa Bay's rushing yardage. He also was the only bright spot of the first half. -- Pat Yasinskas
49ERS 28, COWBOYS 17 | ANALYSIS
Justin Smith. The defensive end's sack in the first quarter forced the Cowboys to settle for a field goal rather than going for the game-tying TD. He finished with six tackles and two sacks. -- Paul Gutierrez
Rolando McClain. DeMarco Murray finished with 118 yards and a touchdown on 22 carries, but his fumble on the opening drive played too big a part in the loss to earn this honor. LB Rolando McClain, coming off two retirements and playing his first game since Nov. 25, 2012, finished with eight tackles. In a physical game, McClain showed up. -- Todd Archer
BRONCOS 31, COLTS 24 | ANALYSIS
Julius Thomas. Another three-touchdown night for Peyton Manning is what many in the seats came to see, but Julius Thomas was the matchup the Broncos exploited early and often. The Colts simply did not have an answer for the Broncos' tight end. -- Jeff Legwold
Reggie Wayne. The veteran receiver eased the mind of the doubters when he continued to show why Andrew Luck relies on him so much. Wayne has at least one catch in 120 straight games and at least three catches in 72 straight games. -- Mike Wells
