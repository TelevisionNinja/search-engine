BC making big plays, still working on timing

September, 7, 2014
Sep 7
3:24
PM ET
By Jack McCluskey | ESPNBoston.com

CHESTNUT HILL, Mass. -- You want more explosive plays, Steve Addazio? You got more explosive plays.

Through two games, Boston College has 28 plays that have produced more than 10 yards (tied for sixth nationally at the start of Saturday’s action). The Eagles have six plays of 20-plus, four plays of 30-plus, three plays of 40-plus and one play of 50-plus yards.

While you might expect that most of those plays came in Week 1 against UMass, that’s not exactly the case. BC had 10 plays of 10-plus, four plays of 20-plus, two plays of 30-plus, two plays of 40-plus and one play of 50-plus yards in its 30-20 loss to Pittsburgh on Friday night.

On BC’s third offensive snap on Friday night, quarterback Tyler Murphy faked an inside handoff to Tyler Rouse, who was immediately swallowed up in the backfield by a Pitt defender.

But Murphy had kept the ball on the read option, and the speedy transfer from Florida shook off a would-be tackler at the line and took off. No. 2 streaked up the left sideline, past the 30, the 20, the 10 and when he got to the 5-yard line and saw safety Terrish Webb angling to keep him out of the end zone he took to the air.

Murphy launched himself toward the left pylon and appeared to hit the orange plastic before he crashed to the maroon turf.

A video review after the play showed his left foot touched down out of bounds before he broke the plane, so the TD had to be punched in from a yard out by Rouse, but the 51-yard run showed just how explosive BC’s new dual-threat QB can be.

Later in the game, with the Panthers in control after ripping off 24 straight points, Murphy made another explosive play. Wideout Shakim Phillips came free over the middle, streaking toward the left sideline and an open end zone. Murphy hit him in stride and the UConn transfer sped over the goal line for a 48-yard TD.

After the game, Addazio was understandably focused on the plays his team didn’t make, including the disastrous offensive series after linebacker Steven Daniels picked off Pitt QB Chad Voytik to set the Eagles up at the Panthers’ 29-yard line.

Offensive coordinator Ryan Day called for three straight passes after the turnover, with the ball falling incomplete each time and the offense producing zero yards before kicker Alex Howell pushed a 46-yard field goal attempt wide right.

“The first one I just missed,” Murphy said afterward. “He was open and it was a great call. The second one, I tried to put it over the ‘backer, the D-line was coming but there’s no excuses. In games like this, you’re expected to make those plays and I didn’t make those today.”

When the Eagles needed to make an explosive play the most, up 7-3 with a chance to build a lead at home and maybe force the visiting Panthers to go away from the run game, they came up empty.

“They were the right calls, they were right on the money,” Addazio said of the sequence of passes, which had Dan Crimmins and Josh Bordner open and didn’t end in completions. “We didn’t connect on the two play-action passes.”

Listening to Addazio after the game was to hear a man of two minds. He wants badly to produce big plays in the passing game, but every missed opportunity through the air seemingly makes him more wary of committing to it in the future. He knows his team should be able to run the ball, and he doesn’t want to take chances away from his offensive line and his stable of running backs.

Someone needs to move the chains and put points on the board.

“Now OK, we threw ‘em and we didn’t get any points out of that drive,” Addazio said. “You gotta hang in there sometimes with the run.”

Phillips, who led the Eagles in catches (four), receiving yards (78) and scores (one) on Friday, said he and his receiving brethren aren’t concerned with the run-pass balance. They’re just trying to do their jobs.

“As players, we don’t really focus on that. That’s the coaches’ job, we’re just there to execute,” he said. “And we didn’t execute as well as we needed to to pull this one out. We just need to be better.”

The Eagles produced a few of the explosive plays Addazio wants so badly to see, but with No. 14 USC coming to town for another national TV game next weekend that is undoubtedly true if they want to avoid falling to 1-2.
