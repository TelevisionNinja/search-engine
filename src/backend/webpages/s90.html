Gonzalez, Kemp power heart of order

September, 7, 2014
Sep 7
6:22
PM PT
By Dan Arritt | ESPNLosAngeles.com

LOS ANGELES -- With much of the pregame conversation focused on Yasiel Puig's drop in the batting order, the Los Angeles Dodgers reminded everyone Sunday afternoon that they're still well represented at the heart of their lineup.

Adrian Gonzalez hit a pair of three-run homers from the No. 3 spot and Matt Kemp homered in the cleanup slot and added the defensive play of the game in right field, throwing out a runner at home in the second inning of the 7-2 victory against the visiting Arizona Diamondbacks.

[+] Enlarge
Jayne Kamin-Oncea/USA TODAY SportsAdrian Gonzalez and Matt Kemp, center, smacked home runs for the Dodgers on Sunday, but it was Gonzalez's six RBIs that was the difference.
The win completed the fourth sweep of the season against the Diamondbacks and gave Los Angeles a season mark of 15-4 against their NL West rival.

"We have so many guys on the team that can hit for power, that can drive in runs, it really doesn't matter where you put us," said Kemp, who followed Gonzalez's second three-run homer with a solo blast in the seventh inning. "Not everybody is going to do good each day, but we always have someone that's going to do something. If we can all put it together at the same time, this is a dangerous team."

The Dodgers showed just how explosive they can be in the sixth inning. Arizona starter Trevor Cahill (3-11) did not allow a hit through 5 1/3 innings but couldn't finish the sixth inning before departing with a 3-2 deficit, thanks to the first three-run blast off the bat of Gonzalez, who matched his career high with six RBIs.

"The two three-run homers were big for us, especially the first one," said Gonzalez, who tallied the 16th multi-homer game of his career.

Gonzalez hit a tailing 3-1 fastball off Cahill for his first homer, holding the bat outstretched in his right hand as he jogged to first and watched the ball sail over the center-field fence.

"I'm looking for the same pitch every pitch from him, fastball middle in," Gonzalez said of Cahill. "He's got so much running away from me. I can't swing at anything that starts away because it's going to run off [the plate]."

Gonzalez's second home run wasn't as obvious, as Diamondbacks center fielder Ender Inciarte crashed into the wall in left center as the ball brushed off the top of the fence. Kemp followed with his 19th homer of the year and No. 5 hitter Andre Ethier was then plunked by Arizona reliever Oliver Perez, drawing a warning from plate umpire Scott Barry, followed by ejections to Dodgers manager Don Mattingly, bench coach Tim Wallach and ace pitcher Clayton Kershaw.

Mattingly and Kemp were surprised that warnings were even issued, considering the pitch that hit Ethier was a slider and not a fastball.

"I don't think any of us thought he [hit Ethier] on purpose," Mattingly said.

Gonzalez's six RBIs also put him over 100 for the season for the seventh time in his career and his 31 home runs against the Diamondbacks are the most of any active player.

"I don't really set goals before the season starts," Gonzalez said. "I try to help our team win every day. I feel if I try and do that, at the end of the year, the numbers are going to be there."

And that's how to stay in the heart of the batting order.
