Man Wanted By Police For Questioning In Stabbing Has Several Past Arrests

Posted 11:07 pm, September 7, 2014, by Ellen Thalls, Updated at 11:11pm, September 7, 2014

Share this:

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Google
  • Pinterest
  • LinkedIn
  • Email

FAYETTEVILLE (KFSM) – Cory Lee Ryan is wanted for questioning after he was seen leaving a hotel room Saturday night where a man was stabbed to death in the throat. Records show Ryan has been arrested several times in the past.

Witnesses described the man leaving the crime scene as thin, wearing sunglasses and no shirt with multiple tattoos and driving a dark passenger vehicle. Police identified that man as the 24-year-old Ryan.

According to court records, he was arrested in June of 2014 on suspicion of aggravated residential burglary. The charges were dropped in the case because of insufficient evidence. Ryan was also arrested in March of 2014 in a theft case. Again, the charges were dropped in the case because of insufficient evidence.

In 2013, Ryan was arrested and pleaded guilty in a domestic battery case. He was given nine months suspended. Five years prior to that, Ryan was arrested in a residential burglary case. He pleaded guilty and was granted work release.

Police also named a second suspect wanted for questioning as an eyewitness to the stabbing. For more information, click here.
