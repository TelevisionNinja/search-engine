Police Looking For Farmington Man In Connection To Deadly Stabbing

Posted 9:52 am, September 7, 2014, by Ellen Thalls and Joshua Peguero, Updated at 11:10pm, September 7, 2014

Share this:

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Google
  • Pinterest
  • LinkedIn
  • Email

FAYETTEVILLE (KFSM) – Fayetteville police are searching for Cory Lee Ryan for questioning after he was spotted at the hotel where a man was stabbed in the throat Saturday night.

Police said the man was taken to Washington Regional Medical Center where he later died from his injuries. The name of the man has not been released, but his body was transported to the Arkansas State Crime Lab for positive identification.

Ryan was last seen driving a dark passenger car on Martin Luther King Boulevard around 8 p.m. Saturday. He was described as wearing sunglasses, no shirt and has multiple tattoos.

Several thousand people were in Fayetteville Saturday for the first Razorback home football game, and some people said it is alarming that a man was killed so close to the stadium.

“I think it would make it more difficult for the police to determine what happened and what the cause was, because you got a lot of other circumstances going on that normally wouldn’t be in place,” Rhonda Davis said.

Davis came from Kansas City for the game and said the news was unsettling. Police found out about the stabbing because a witness ran to the intersection of Martin Luther King Blvd. and Futrall Dr. to tell an officer directing game day traffic.

“I just think it’s really shocking and sad that someone lost their life, and the fact that that person is still out there and they haven’t gotten them yet is scary,” Davis said.

Texas native Collin Henderson also was in town for the game. He said he may think twice about coming to another football game if his family’s safety is in jeopardy.

“It’s concerning for sure, I mean I have a daughter, so that’s scary to think somebody’s out there trying to hurt people,” Henderson said.

Ryan has been arrested several times in the past six years. For more information, click here. Anyone with information on Ryan’s whereabouts should call Fayetteville Police at 479-587-3555.
