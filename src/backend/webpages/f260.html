Friday, Sept. 5

Quiksilver shares tumble to near 5-year low(11:48 am ET)
NEW YORK (MarketWatch) -- Quiksilver shares (ZQK: news, chart, profile) plunged 30% to a near five-year low, but Janney Capital analyst Eric Tracy said that's still not a big enough haircut to make them attractive. Shares of the surfing and skateboarding apparel maker, which reported late Thursday a surprise fiscal third-quarter loss and missed sales expectations by a wide margin, have lost 77% so far this year, compared with an 8% gain in the S&P 500. Tracy said the results confirmed his concerns about secular changes in the action sports market, which have been exacerbated by a competitive retail environment and migration to online sales. "The recent share weakness and post-quarter pullback helps risk/reward...but [near-term] catalysts remain relatively limited, while [long-term] uncertainty remains," Tracy wrote in a note to clients. He cut his fair value estimate for the shares to $2.50 from $4. The stock was trading at $1.97 midday Friday, the lowest price seen since Dec. 22, 2009.

SAN FRANCISCO (MarketWatch) -- Mexican-style restaurant chain El Pollo Loco Holdings Inc. (LOCO: news, chart, profile) , in its first quarterly update to shareholders, reported a fiscal second-quarter net profit of $6.6 million, or 21 cents a share. Gap shares fall after surprise decline in August same-store sales(4:17 pm ET)
NEW YORK (MarketWatch) -- Shares of Gap Inc. (GPS: news, chart, profile) dropped 5.6% in after-hours trade Thursday, after the apparel retailer reported disappointing sales for August. Same-store sales, or sales of stores open over a year, fell 2% from year-earlier levels, missing analyst expectations of a 1.6% rise, according to Thomson Reuters I/B/E/S estimates. Total sales for the month were flat at $1.23 billion. Within Gap's brands, Banana Republic global same-store sales declined 2% and Old Navy global same-store sales increased 2%, compared with expectations of increases of 0.7% and 3.4%, respectively. "We're encouraged by the positive momentum at Old Navy, while focused on the necessary steps to drive improved performance across our portfolio of brands in the back half," said Chief Executive Glenn Murphy. The stock, which closed Thursday at the highest level since April 4, 2000, has gained 19% year to date while the S&P 500 has added 8.1%.

U.S. nonmanufacturing firms see faster growth in August: ISM(10:12 am ET)
WASHINGTON (MarketWatch) -- U.S. services and other nonmanufacturing companies reported faster growth for August, with a gauge of their activity hitting the highest pace since 2008, according to a survey of senior executives released Thursday. The Institute for Supply Management said its gauge of nonmanufacturers rose to 59.6% last month from 58.7% in July. Economists polled by MarketWatch had expected the August reading to pull back to 57.2%. Readings over 50% indicate an overall expansion. Of the industries tracked by Tempe, Ariz.-based ISM, 15 reported growth last month, while two reported contractions. Also Thursday, ISM reported that its gauge of new orders for nonmanufacturers showed slower growth in August, hitting 63.8%, down 1.1 points from July. Meanwhile, the employment barometer rose 1.1 points to 57.1%, and the gauge of business activity/production rose 2.6 points to 65%.
