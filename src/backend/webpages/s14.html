Cowboys cure 49ers' preseason ills

September, 7, 2014
Sep 7
10:15
PM ET
By Paul Gutierrez | ESPN.com

ARLINGTON, Texas -- Whatever ailed the San Francisco 49ers in the preseason -- the offense lacked rhythm, and the defense had more questions than answers -- the Dallas Cowboys provided the perfect elixir in a 28-17 victory Sunday.

And then some, considering the way the Cowboys kept shooting themselves in the foot early, with four first-half turnovers.

On the first snap of the game, Cowboys left tackle Tyron Smith committed a false start.

On the third, 49ers linebacker Dan Skuta, starting in place of the suspended Aldon Smith, stripped the ball from DeMarco Murray, and Chris Culliver’s scoop and score -- he went 35 yards -- essentially ended the game.

[+] Enlarge
Christian Petersen/Getty ImagesPatrick Willis' end-zone interception was just one example of how the 49ers benefited from the Cowboys' generosity.
Or did you not notice that the Cowboys held a massive time advantage of 13:26 to 1:34 at the end of the first quarter, yet the 49ers were leading where it counted most: on the scoreboard, 21-3?

It was a lead that would grow to 28-3 before the half.

"We have to keep going," 49ers quarterback Colin Kaepernick said. "They have a great offense. They can score points at any moment, so offensively we need to score again."

The Cowboys, however, obliged a defense already missing three starters in Smith, nose tackle Glenn Dorsey and inside linebacker NaVorro Bowman.

Besides Murray’s early fumble, Tony Romo threw three interceptions, one in the end zone to Patrick Willis.

"It was one of those things where you couldn’t believe he actually threw it," said Willis, who now has eight career picks and scored his first since 2012. "My eyes got really big, and the ball got really small. I was thinking, ‘Just hold onto it.’ It felt good."

A touchdown would have pulled the Cowboys within 21-10 early in the second quarter. Instead ...

How off was Romo early? Three of his four incompletions at halftime were interceptions.

"There were some great plays -- a lot of great plays -- a lot of great team play and individual play," 49ers coach Jim Harbaugh said. "I looked around the locker room after the game and realized all the young guys that stepped up in this game. Then, all the veteran players, guys that had been there before, done it before, got the battle scars. They truly led the way, and there was some great play."

It was the type of play sorely missing in the preseason, when the 49ers first-string offense managed but two field goals in seven possessions. And yet, with the Cowboys returning a defense that was historically bad in 2013 -- they allowed the third-most yards in league history -- there was a sense that going against a unit that had Oakland Raiders draft bust Rolando McClain starting at middle linebacker would jumpstart the 49ers.

Although the 49ers offense was not exactly a well-oiled machine -- the Cowboys actually led in first downs (26-19) and yards (382-316) and had fewer penalties (10 for 72 yards, compared to the 49ers’ 11 for 80 yards) -- it was more than enough.

"We got the win, but we have to clean stuff up, especially in the running game," said running back Frank Gore, who led the 49ers with 63 yards on the ground. "But we got the win, and that’s the most important thing."
