ARLINGTON, Texas -- Derek Holland doesn't play the what-if game.

He's not going there with you and doesn't care to review what might have been if he hadn't tripped over his dog at the top of those steps last winter.

[+] Enlarge
Jim Cowsert/USA TODAY SportsDerek Holland has worked all season to get back on a major-league mound, and Sunday he showed how effective he can be when healthy.
It's not a joking matter about what happened to the Texas Rangers' left-handed starter. He suffered a severe knee injury requiring microfacture surgery.

"You don't predict anything like that with the accident," Holland said. "That happened but the main thing was I wanted to come out and prove I could bounce back and all the hard work I've been doing is going to pay off."

Rapid Reaction: Rangers 1, Mariners 0

ARLINGTON, Texas -- Imagine if that dog hadn't been trying to play around with Derek Holland at the top of the stairs this winter.

The Rangers' starting rotation has been a mess with inconsistency and injuries.

Holland was recovering from left knee surgery and wanted to return. He didn't care that the Rangers were on pace to lose 100 games. He just wanted to help out.

He wanted to let everyone know not to forget about him and the rehab starts in Round Rock and Frisco meant something to him.

Sunday afternoon, the left-hander pitched wonderfully against a playoff-caliber team with seven shutout innings in the Rangers' 1-0 victory over the Seattle Mariners.

After proving to the front office he can field his position and could command his pitches, Holland is finally back.

Yes, it's too late, but as general manager Jon Daniels tries to figure out what to do from finding a manager to fixing the rotation and lineup, Holland wants to be crossed off the list of things about which to worry.

Holland is letting the Rangers know he's a dependable pitcher for 2015.

Sunday he struck out five and gave up six hits in 93 pitches as the Rangers picked up their fifth 1-0 victory of the season, matching a franchise record.

How it happened?: A scoreless game turned in the sixth inning when Adrian Beltre drove in Leonys Martin on a sacrifice fly for the only run.

Martin started the inning with a single, stole second and advanced to third on Daniel Robertson's ground out. Beltre brought him in for the only score Holland would need.

The end of the losing: The Rangers ended a season-high-tying eight-game losing streak with the victory. It came with a few tense moments in the ninth inning as closer Neftali Feliz got out of a jam with runners at the corners and one out. He got James Jones to pop out to short and retired Justin Smoak on a flyout to left. It was Tim Bogar's first victory as a manager.

Lineup, lineup, lineup: Bogar moved Martin back to the top of the order against a left-handed pitcher, this time starter James Paxton. Martin had three hits and a run scored in the effort. Bogar said before the game he wants to see if Martin can be the everyday center fielder against left-handed pitching.

Bogar also had his third different No. 3 man in the order, this time it was Adam Rosales, who had a hit and walk in three trips.

What's next: The Rangers have an off day before starting a three-game series with the Los Angeles Angels on Tuesday.

ARLINGTON, Texas -- Texas Rangers interim manager Tim Bogar is doing something the previous man who held his position didn’t do: Play center fielder Leonys Martin against left-handed pitching.

Ron Washington, who resigned on Friday for personal reasons, didn’t trust Martin against lefties. On the season, Martin is hitting just .218 against left-handed pitching.

Yet, Martin was in the leadoff spot on Sunday against the Seattle Mariners. As a leadoff hitter, Martin is hitting .329 in 73 at-bats. The Rangers normally use Shin-Soo Choo as the leadoff hitter, but he’s out for the season recovering from bone chips in his elbow.

“I want to see Marty play,” Bogar said. “I want to know that he’s going to be our everyday center fielder next year. I want to see him play against the lefties and I want to see him face the lefties and come in like last night against (Charlie Furbush). I think we need to know if we need another guy to play center field against lefties or not.”

Robbie Ross is out: LHP Robbie Ross has been taken out of the rotation. Ross gave up six runs, all earned, in 1 2/3 innings in Thursday’s loss to Seattle.

“I don’t have a choice,” Ross said. “I’d like to be a starter. I feel like that’s where I want to be. If this is what they choose for me, I will do the best that I can.”

With Ross out of the rotation, Colby Lewis, Nick Tepesch and Nick Martinez will start the three-game series with the Angels this week. Derek Holland, Sunday’s starter, will face Atlanta on Friday. Scott Baker will pitch next Saturday in Game 2 of the Braves series.

Do it for Durrett: The benefit concern for former ESPNDallas Rangers’ beat writer, Richard Durrett, who passed away in June, is Monday at Billy Bob’s Texas. Go here to buy tickets. Also, a recent item put up for auction is a signed helmet and football from Auburn football coach Gus Malzahn.

Long-time minor leaguer gets callup: Guilder Rodriguez was called up from Double-A Frisco on Sunday. To make room on the 40-man roster, Yu Darvish was moved to the 60-day disabled list. Rodriguez has played 1,095 games in the minors spanning 13 season.

“Every single day, I play hard and everyday I waited for this opportunity,” Rodriguez said.

Injury update: Catcher Robinson Chirinos (neck) is still having problems throwing and probably needs a few more days. ... LF Jake Smolinski (on DL with fractured foot) will undergo a CT scan Tuesday to see if he can be cleared for baseball activities. ... RHP Miles Mikolas (sore shoulder) is on target to start a throwing program this week.

ARLINGTON, Texas -- Seeing his players fail in situational hitting situations in the first three innings of Saturday’s 4-2 loss to Seattle did not please Texas Rangers interim manager Tim Bogar.

And a lineup littered with young players is not an excuse.

“The guys know what they’re supposed to do, and they’re capable of doing it," Bogar said at his postgame media conference. “We need to have better at-bats. We need to execute better."

Leonys Martin reached second base with none out in both the first and third innings. Needing a ground ball to the right side to move Martin to third, Elvis Andrus instead struck out in each instance.

The Rangers had runners at first and third with none out in the second inning after a single by Tomas Telis. Following a strikeout by Ryan Rua, Luis Sardinas and Michael Choice flied out.

The Rangers were 0-for-9 with runners in scoring position in the first three innings. They finished 0-for-10.

Texas did not capitalize on a solid pitching performance from starter Nick Martinez.

“Early in the game, Nick was making the pitches he needed to make to get the double play in the second inning," Bogar said. “He was mixing his pitches and keeping [the Mariners] off balance. You could see his confidence growing. He kept us in the game."

One pitch spoiled Martinez's night. Kyle Seager drove a 1-0 pitch over the right-field wall with Robinson Cano on base.

Reliever Neal Cotts was touched for two runs in the eighth inning and fell to 2-9 for the season. His ERA, which was 3.20 16 games ago, has climbed to 4.18.

“The other teams don’t seem to be waiting around," Bogar said. “They are going right after him."

ARLINGTON, Texas -- Two days on the job and Texas Rangers interim manager Tim Bogar is still looking for his first victory.

Actually, given that Saturday’s rain-delayed start did not end until after 1:18 a.m. CT Sunday, Bogar was into his third day by the finish.

The Seattle Mariners clipped Rangers reliever Neal Cotts for three consecutive singles and two runs in the eighth inning for a 4-2 victory before an announced crowd of 29,552 at Globe Life Park.

Cotts entered with a 2-2 score and began the eighth by walking substitute left fielder Chris Denorfia. After getting Robinson Cano on a fly to left, Kyle Seager, Kendrys Morales and Logan Morrison lined base hits. The latter two drove in runs.

The defeat extended the Rangers’ losing streak to eight, matching their longest of the season.

A few observations:

Quality start: Nick Martinez received no decision after working six innings, allowing six hits and two runs, the damage coming on a sixth-inning home run by Rangers-killer Kyle Seager that tied the score.

Seager’s home run traveled an estimated 377 feet into the lower porch of the right-field stands. The homer was his 23rd of the season and his fifth home run off Texas pitching.

Perhaps belying his 3-10 record, Martinez put together his fourth solid outing in his past six starts.


Rangers runs: Texas broke through with two unearned runs off Seattle starter and Highland Park, Texas, product Chris Young in the fourth inning.

Ryan Rua's RBI double scored Tomas Telis, who reached on an error by Seattle shortstop Brad Miller. Rua, who finished with three hits, went to third on a sacrifice bunt by Luis Sardinas and scored on a sacrifice fly by Michael Choice.

Wet but playable: The grounds crew did excellent work to get Globe Life Park playable after almost four hours of steady rain. The official delay was three hours, 13 minutes when the first pitch was finally delivered at 10:18 p.m.

Trending up: Coming off a three-hit game Friday, Leonys Martin led off the bottom of the first for the Rangers with a bunt hit and promptly stole second. He followed it up with a lined single to right to start the third and another steal. Martin, making his 16th start in the leadoff spot, came in batting .375 over his 22 previous games. The Rangers, however, fell to 2-14 with Martin hitting leadoff.

Not so clutch: Hitting with mates in scoring position continues to plague Texas. In the first three innings, the Rangers were 0-for-9, with four strikeouts and four weak popups. Just a productive out, perhaps choking up to hit a ground ball to the right side to move a runner instead of a two-strike home-run swing, might help get the job done.

Up next: The Rangers and Mariners conclude their four-game series at 2:05 p.m. Sunday with left-hander Derek Holland (0-0, 1.29 ERA) making his second start of an injury-plagued season. He will be opposed by lefty James Paxton (5-1, 1.91) on ESPN 103.3 FM and Fox Sports Southwest.

ARLINGTON, Texas -- With his first day as Texas Rangers interim manager behind him, expect to see more smiles on the face of Tim Bogar.

Those who know Bogar well conveyed to him the message that he needs to smile more and let his personality come out.

Friday’s unexpected resignation of Ron Washington was not a happy occasion for anyone in a Rangers uniform. The capper to a shocking day, a 7-5 loss to the Seattle Mariners, was Texas’ seventh consecutive defeat.

Bogar shared more of his philosophy in an extended media session before Saturday’s scheduled game against Seattle at Globe Life Park.

“The common thread for the successful managers I’ve been around is they care about their players," Bogar said. “You have to get the players’ trust before you get their respect. The most important thing for me right now is to establish a relationship with the players [and get] on the same page going forward."

Although Bogar said he does not plan to manage strictly by the mounds of statistics at the disposal of all clubs, he values them.

“You are a fool if you don’t use the numbers," the 47-year-old former big leaguer said. "I understand they are valuable, but it is still up to me to make the decision."

He said his predecessor sometimes went against the numbers.

“I think what Ron trusted were his eyes -- 44 years of experience," Bogar said.

Bogar dealt with many phone calls and text messages on his first day.

“Every single one wanted to make sure Wash was OK," Bogar said.

Because he wears the interim tag, Bogar was asked if these next three weeks will be looked upon as his audition.

“It’s not about me becoming a manager," Bogar said. “It is more about the guys in the locker room. ... Do I want to do it? Of course I do."

Because he has been Washington’s right-hand man this season, Bogar sees his familiarity with the organization as an advantage compared to “coming in cold."

“I love the game ... I love being a part of it," he said. “[Playing] baseball is a difficult thing to do. I always wanted to be the guy that helps guys succeed."

Lineup moves: Bogar made a pair of interesting moves with his Saturday lineup. Leonys Martin will be elevated to the leadoff spot for just the 16th time this season, and Rougned Odor will bat third as designated hitter. Odor has batted no higher than sixth in his previous 89 starts.

Rain delay: Saturday's scheduled 7:05 p.m. start with the Seattle Mariners is being pushed back by a rain delay. The game is set to begin 45 minutes after the rain stops.

ARLINGTON, Texas -- The Texas Rangers' Friday started with a 3 p.m. meeting, described as emotional, during which Ron Washington announced his resignation as team manager.

Then the business of baseball continued on the field in the evening, and the Rangers were beaten 7-5 by the playoff-contending Seattle Mariners.

Scott Baker lasted just 4⅓ innings, during which he gave up three home runs: a two-run shot to Kendrys Morales in the second inning before a solo shot to Mike Zunino and a two-run blast from Brad Miller in the fifth.

It was a difficult debut for interim manager Tim Bogar, but the Rangers (53-88) had been struggling with Washington running the show.

The club has lost seven consecutive games and has yet to win in September.

What happened? Do you really want to know? Really? OK, Kyle Seager drew a leadoff walk in the second and Morales homered into the right-field seats for a 2-0 lead.

The Rangers cut the deficit to one in the fourth on Rougned Odor's RBI single. Pitching coach Mike Maddux wanted to see a shutdown inning from Baker and he didn’t get it. With one out, Zunino homered to right-center and, after a single and stolen base from Endy Chavez, Miller homered to push the lead to 5-1.

Bogar replaced Baker with Shawn Tolleson.

The Rangers were down 7-2 when Adam Rosales' fourth home run of the season cut the deficit to three in the sixth. Leonys Martin scored on a wild pitch in the ninth to make things interesting for Seattle closer Fernando Rodney.

Interim status: Bogar becomes the fifth interim manager in club history. Del Wilber (1973), Connie Ryan (1977), Toby Harrah (1992) and Jerry Narron (2001) were the others. With Bogar the interim manager, Bobby Jones takes over as the bench coach.

First challenge: Bogar challenged a 6-4-3 double play in the eighth when it appeared Michael Choice was safe at first, but a review confirmed the umpires' call. The Rangers are 18-for-36 on challenges this season.

Rios out: Right fielder Alex Rios, who has been dealing with a swollen right thumb the past few weeks, has developed an infection. Team officials said he’ll miss at least a week. Rios, who has underachieved this season, is hitting just .184 in the past 28 games.

Minor league success: Myrtle Beach defeated Salem 5-0 on Friday night to advance to the championship series of the Carolina League, winning the best-of-three Southern Division series 2-1. Royce Bolinger drove in two runs and Chad James struck out seven over 5⅔ innings for the victory. Myrtle Beach will host Potomac in Game 1 of the best-of-five championship series beginning Saturday.

What’s next? Nick Martinez (3-10, 5.14 ERA) takes on fellow right-hander Chris Young (12-7, 3.46), a Highland Park, Texas, native, in a scheduled 7:05 p.m. start Saturday.

ARLINGTON, Texas – The next Texas Rangers manager must be someone who is disposed to sabermetrics, can deal with a young clubhouse and has the ability to guide a club with high expectations coming off what might be a 100-loss season.

“As soon as we can find somebody,” Rangers co-chairman Ray Davis said Friday, after the resignation of Ron Washington sent the organization into a frenzy.

The Rangers had been expecting Washington to return in 2015, the final year of his contract. But that changed in recent weeks, with Washington dealing with a personal issue that moved him to discuss his options with club officials.

Those close to Washington said they had no clue what the issue might be. General manager Jon Daniels said Washington’s issue wasn’t related to drugs, for which Washington tested positive in 2009.

One source said Washington doesn’t have a health issue.

It was interesting to hear the comments from Davis and fellow Rangers co-owner Bob Simpson.

Each praised Washington’s spirit, but neither said anything about his ability to manage a club on the field.

“There is some sadness and I care a lot for the man -- he brought a collegiate atmosphere to professional sport, which we all enjoyed,” Simpson said. “He endeared himself to his players and the fans and to ownership and to management -- and we’re going to miss that. I was looking forward to more time with him.”

Interim manager Tim Bogar will get the final 22 games -- including Friday's against the Seattle Mariners -- to make his case; pitching coach Mike Maddux, in the final year of his contract, will get serious consideration.

With the Houston Astros also looking for a manager and given the ties Maddux has with Nolan Ryan -- now an adviser for the Astros -- he could get an opportunity in both locations.

Steve Buechele, manager of the Rangers' Triple-A Round Rock affiliate, is another possibility, and of course the Rangers will look outside the organization.

“You finally get your chance to manage a team and obviously this is an opportunity, for a small, brief window, to build trust with the players and build trust with the front office and ownership and do things the correct way,” Bogar said. “I just believe I’ve been around enough managers to do it the right way, who have taught me enough so that when this opportunity did come, I’ll be prepared for it.”

Meet the interim manager: Tim Bogar

ARLINGTON, Texas -- With Ron Washington gone as Texas Rangers manager, Tim Bogar becomes the interim replacement, getting his first chance to take over the club full-time.

Bogar was in his first season as Rangers bench coach.

"Tim is well regarded, and he has the respect of his peers and players," Rangers general manager Jon Daniels said Friday. "Hard-working, organized, knowledgeable about the game, respect among the staff -- and he was a natural choice to manage the club down the stretch."

Bogar was a minor league manager for three different franchises, compiling a 362-266 mark. In his five seasons, Bogar's teams reach the league's championship round four times.

[+] Enlarge
Tom Pennington/Getty ImagesTim Bogar, who compiled a 362-266 record in five seasons managing in the minor leagues, takes over in the wake of Ron Washington's resignation.
He was named manager of the year in 2004 with the Houston Astros' rookie league team and in 2006 with the Cleveland Indians' Double-A squad.

"You finally get your chance to manage a team, and obviously this is an opportunity, for a small, brief window, to build trust with the players and build trust with the front office and ownership and do things the correct way," said Bogar, who was the Boston Red Sox bench coach in 2012. "I just believe, I've been around enough managers to do it the right way who have taught me enough so that when this opportunity did come I'll be prepared for it."

Bogar played nine seasons in the big leagues, for the New York Mets, Astros and Los Angeles Dodgers. Bogar, a career .228 hitter, was an eighth-round draft pick by the Mets in 1987.

Whether or not Bogar keeps the job is uncertain.

Rangers pitching coach Mike Maddux is in the final year of his contract and will get consideration for the gig, along with several other candidates, possibly including Steve Buechele, manager of the Rangers' Triple-A Round Rock club.

One thing notable about Bogar's first lineup Friday night was his decision to move rookie catcher Tomas Telis, a switch hitter, to No. 3 in the lineup.

"I'm sure the front office will consider Bogey for next year and stuff like that," pitcher Colby Lewis said. "I know Bogey will step in and do a great job, and we’re going to play these last 22 games like Wash would want us, and that’s about it."
